About

Meme- an image (or multiple moving ones-a video) that has been copied and spread after being transformed from its original state. Whether the transformation is a simple caption or a distortion of the image, its presence is what elevates an image to the status of a meme.

This is a journey to meet three different people and learn from their experiences with memes. Helping me in this journey is Dr. Wojak Kowalski- the Head of Department of Meme Culture and GenZ Studies at the Memebridge School of Arts and Humanities, Yes Chad- A GenZ self-proclaimed Memelord and chief editor for Meme Insider, and finally Incel Boomer- a father of 4 and proud Monster Energy Zero Ultra addict.

Join us in the next episode of “Box Explained” where we investigate birds and convince you that they’re all just government-controlled drones.

The Team

Memes Explained

Yes Chad

Yes Chad is one of the Earth’s finest meme creators. His role as the chief editor of Meme Insider makes him the de facto leader of meme enthusiasts around the world. His influence over international policies has made many “Boomers” wary of his position and the overall power of memes on the whole. His comments regarding meme “normies” was the biggest controversy of 2013, a full thousand reacts higher than the story of NSA whistleblower Edward Snowden (Stonks Man et al., 2013).

Incel Boomer is the first 55 year old we caught hold of when filming this episode of Box Explained. His greatest accomplishment was winning the argument with his now ex-wife Karen to name their 4th child Kyle. Upon questioning, Kyle does not seem to have come to terms with her name even until her 20th birthday. Incel Boomer also gets very defensive when asked about his Monster Energy Zero Ultra drinking habits.

Incel Boomer

Dr. Wojak Kowalski

Dr. Wojak Kowalski is the current Head of Department of Meme Culture and Gen Z studies at the Memebridge School of Arts and Humanities. His research revolves around the influence of memes on individual personalities as well as the state of public discourse. His Ph.D thesis involved an in-depth analysis of Rage comics and the delineation of the Arab Spring as a result of the “Trololo face” meme. He is currently in the process of writing his 3rd book which describes how the “Pepe the Frog” meme led to Donald Trump’s victory in the 2016 US presidential election.
